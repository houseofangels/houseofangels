Program

Residents will learn of the disease of addiction/alcoholism which are goal and task oriented, using the Big Book of Alcoholics Anonymous and the disease model of addiction. Giving them skills to break the cycle of addiction. Our program is 12-step based.

  • Work on Relapse Prevention with informative relapse factors and practical guidelines to assist in the prevention of relapses. Each resident will demonstrate knowledge and skill in outlined areas which will be presented at each workshop.
  • Health Awareness/nutritional workshops will be ongoing to aid in recovery by helping them to make better choices for their well being.
  • Motivational groups weekly be ongoing as this is important helping residents to build confidence and self esteem
  • Discussion/Dinner community meetings in house will help residents to learn interactions with each other in a family setting enhancing communication skills with other residents that will help them as they interact together in a safe setting; to going out into the public setting.
  • Job Search skills and application processes will enhance residents abilities to get back into the work force and regaining responsibility
  • GED education to aid in residents moving forward in a career/job as needed
  • Residents will benefit from getting involved in activities that help them physically, socially and mentally, spiritually, accommodating the needs of each personality.
  • Counseling that will aid in restoration of family, loved ones by learning, then changing the patterns that continued to lead them down the same destructive paths. Thus then being able to put into action a new life by acquiring new skills and tools to do so.
    Attending in house and outside AA meetings, or other groups as approved by staff.
