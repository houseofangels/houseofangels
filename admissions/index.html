Admissions

Staff at the Angel House of Georgia screen potential residents confidentially either by telephone or in a personal interview to determine the potential individual√≠s eligibility to enter the program. In taking the first step towards recovery potential residents please contact our office directly at 770-572-7945 to complete a phone screening. Payment will be discussed privately.

An orientation will be given to residents and families /loved ones upon intake approval by staff. Our Orientation includes overview of the program, its stages, levels that they will achieve, rules, group schedules, routines each day, staff/client expectations, activities included, resources for the families of the resident. Only with approved release of information will orientations be given to residents and families or loved ones together.

Requirements for the Angel House of Georgia

  1. Be a woman 18 years of age or older.
  2. Be free from alcohol or substance abuse for at least 72 hours and not in need of detoxification.
  3. Must submit to a urine/saliva drug screen upon admission.
  4. Must not have any active warrants in this or any other county
  5. Must no have ever had a sexual charge; we reserve the right to do background check.
  6. Must not be HIV positive; failure to disclose information results I immediate termination
  7. Be entering our facility voluntarily or court mandated as approved to be at our facility by the court.
  8. Must have documentation showing they have been tested for TB in the past 6 months, or be tested prior to entering the program; these tests are determined to be negative for active tuberculosis infection; and the individual is not contagious as determined by a physician.
  9. Be medically stable.
  10. Have adequate control over her behavior and assessed to be not dangerous to self or others.
  11. Express a desire to recover from her addiction to alcohol or drugs.
  12. Be assessed as medically appropriate and free of any illness or infection that requires isolation from others.
  13. Have the capacity for active participation in all levels of the program.
  14. To be able to meet personal needs without assistance.

The person needing treatment services:
Must recognize that alcohol/drug abuse is a problem and expresses a desire to change.
